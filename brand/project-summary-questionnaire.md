# Project Summary Questionnaire

Use this once per project. The goal is to collect enough detail to write a sharp portfolio summary now and a stronger case study later.

## How To Use This

- Answer in plain bullets, not polished prose.
- If you do not know an exact metric, give a directional estimate and label it.
- If a question does not apply, mark it `N/A`.
- Prioritize business context, user impact, and your specific contribution.

### 2. Business Context

- What business problem did this project exist to solve?
- Why did it matter to the company?
- What was at risk if nothing changed?
- Was this tied to revenue, cost savings, speed, reliability, compliance, customer experience, or something else?
- Who funded or pushed for the work?

### 3. User Problem

- Who were the primary users?
- What were they trying to get done?
- What was frustrating, slow, confusing, or error-prone before this project?
- How often did this problem happen?
- What did a successful outcome look like for the user?

### 4. Scope And Role

- What part of the project did you personally own?
- What decisions were you responsible for?
- What did other teammates own?
- Did you work with product, design, operations, QA, or leadership?
- Were you brought in early to shape the solution or later to execute it?

### 5. Solution Summary

- What did you build or improve?
- What changed in the product, workflow, or system?
- What made this solution better than the previous approach?
- What were the most important features or capabilities?
- If you had to explain the solution in two sentences to a hiring manager, what would you say?

### 6. Technical Shape

- Frontend stack:
- Backend stack:
- Data layer:
- Cloud / infrastructure:
- Integrations / APIs:
- Anything technically unusual, difficult, or high-stakes?

### 7. Constraints And Tradeoffs

- What constraints shaped the project?
- Did you face legacy systems, limited time, unclear requirements, small team size, budget limits, hardware limits, or compliance rules?
- What tradeoffs did you make intentionally?
- What did you decide not to build, and why?

### 8. Execution Details

- What were the hardest implementation challenges?
- What bugs, edge cases, or reliability problems had to be solved?
- What technical decisions had the biggest downstream impact?
- Did you improve performance, stability, maintainability, or developer workflow?
- If applicable, what parts required cross-team coordination?

### 9. Outcomes And Evidence

- What measurable outcomes came from the work?
- Did it increase revenue, reduce errors, cut manual work, improve speed, or raise adoption?
- What metrics do you have?
- If no hard metrics exist, what strong qualitative evidence do you have?
- Did stakeholders, users, or teammates give notable feedback?

### 10. Differentiators

- What makes this project worth showing in your portfolio?
- What does it prove about how you work?
- What skills does it demonstrate beyond the tech stack?
- Why would a hiring manager remember this project?

### 11. Your Reflection

- What are you most proud of?
- What would you do differently with more time?
- What did you learn from this project?
- Did this project change how you think about product, engineering, or users?

### 12. Portfolio Assets

- Do you have screenshots, diagrams, code snippets, or photos?
- Is there anything confidential that must be omitted or generalized?
- Can the company name be shown publicly?
- Can specific metrics be shown publicly?
- Is there a safe public-facing way to describe the project if details are sensitive?
